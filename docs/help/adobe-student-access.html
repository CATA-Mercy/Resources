Music Production & Recording Arts · Mercy University

Sign in & install Adobe apps in the labs

Follow these steps to sign in with your Mercy credentials and make sure Adobe recognizes your student license on lab computers.

1. Open Creative Cloud on a lab Mac

On a lab computer, go to Applications and launch Adobe Creative Cloud. If an Adobe app (like Photoshop or Premiere) is already open, quit it first.

  1. Click the Creative Cloud icon in the Dock or open it from Applications.
  2. If it asks you to sign in, continue to the next step.

2. Sign in with your Mercy email

When the sign-in window appears, always start with your Mercy email address (not a personal Gmail or previous account).

  1. Enter your Mercy email address (for example, firstname.lastname@mercy.edu).
  2. If asked to choose an account type, select Company or School account.
  3. You’ll be redirected to the Mercy single sign-on page — sign in with your usual Mercy login.
Tip: If you accidentally sign in with a personal Adobe ID, sign out from the profile menu in the top right of Creative Cloud and try again using your Mercy account.

3. Launch the app you need

Once Creative Cloud shows you as signed in, you can open any app that is installed on the lab machine.

  1. In Creative Cloud, go to the Apps tab.
  2. Look for the app you need (for example, Photoshop, Illustrator, or Premiere Pro).
  3. Click Open next to the app name. You should not see a trial message on lab computers.

Lab Macs already have the approved apps installed — you don’t need to run a full install each time.

4. If you see a trial or “no license” message

If Creative Cloud says you’re on a trial, or an app asks you to start a trial or buy a license:

  1. Confirm you are signed in with your Mercy email (Company/School account).
  2. Sign out of Creative Cloud completely, quit the app, then sign back in with your Mercy account.
  3. Try opening the app again from the Creative Cloud app list.
If the issue continues on multiple lab machines, take a screenshot of the error and contact the MPRA team so we can check your Adobe entitlement.

5. When you’re finished

Before you leave a lab Mac, make sure your work is saved and your account is signed out:

  1. Save your work to your external drive or approved cloud storage.
  2. Quit all Adobe apps.
  3. Optional but recommended: open Creative Cloud, click your profile, and choose Sign out.
  4. Log out of the Mac itself.
